Bitcoin is a digital commodity that has a limited supply of 21 millions. Bitcoins come about through a process called mining. These features are comparable to gold, leading to the often cited digital gold.

Since 2009, it has grown in value 17,500%. In 2009, the price of bitcoin was 1 KES. It went up to an All time high of 1163,000 KES in December 2013. As it finds use cases as a store of value, medium of exchange and increasingly, a unit of account, the price of bitcoin goes up too.

Today, bitcoin is used for making online payments, sending and receiving remittances, and cross border business payments. It also traded as an asset class in regulated financial markets: A Bitcoin Electronic Trade Note in Stockholm, Sweden, Wall Street’s Publicly tradable Bitcoin Investment and Hedge funds.

As the number of bitcoins going into supply diminishes and Bitcoin and the blockchain find utility in business, the price is expected to go up.

In Kenya, the easiest way to safely invest in bitcoin is to buy some for yourself and speculate on its future potential. In other countries such as the United States and Sweden regulation has made it possible to get easy and secure exposure to bitcoin. Mutual trust funds, like the publicly traded Bitcoin Investment Trust and Exchange Traded Notes on Sweden’s Nasdaq Exchange.

When choosing to invest in bitcoin, you can choose to acquire bitcoin directly, or indirectly via an investment vehicle, depending on your options.

A Bitcoin exchange is an online market where buyers and sellers meet to trade bitcoin. It is similar to other types of exchanges such as stock exchanges, commodity and currency exchanges. Bitcoin exchanges however, facilitate the trade of Bitcoin and cryptocurrencies.

At Bitcoin exchanges, customers typically go through a signup and verification process before they are allowed to deposit funds to beginning trading.

Typically, at an exchange sellers will list their asking price and buyers will make bids. Both are listed on a market order book with bids and asks on each side of the order books.

Before signing up for services at an exchange, it is recommended to conduct a thorough research on the exchange.

Here is a list of some reputable exchanges around the world. There is no bitcoin exchange in Kenya as of July 2016.

  • Kraken (Europe)
  • Bitstamp (Europe)
  • Coinbase (US)
  • BTCC (China)
  • BitFinex (Hong Kong)

You can view trading activity on all exchanges at Bitcoinwisdom.com

IF you took steps to secure your bitcoins and backed up your wallet, your coins can be recovered.
