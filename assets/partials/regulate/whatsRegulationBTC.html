Paper money is legal tender such as the Kenya shilling or US dollar, are issued by the authority of the national government, through the Central Bank. The government only accepts legal tender for payment of taxes due to the state. Additionally, paper money exists in physical form, like cash.

Bitcoin, was designed to work differently. It was inspired by the reckless monetary functions exercised by banks, governments, dictators and central banks in the financial crisis of 2008. Therefore, it has no central authority and is said to be decentralized. Instead of a single entity or person controlling the money supply, bitcoin is issued by a transparent consensus process. Bitcoin does not exist in physical form, it is a purely online currency

Bitcoin is a purely digital currency, it exists only online; there is no paper version of bitcoin. This is different from traditional forms of currency where both paper and electronic forms exist together at the same time. For example a 100 Kenya shilling note and 100 Kenya Shillings on Mpesa.

The two are also distinguishable by how they issued, that is, how they come about.

Regular currency is printed by a countryâ€™s central monetary authority, the Central Bank. In Kenya, the Central Bank for example, controls monetary policy like how much money is printed, prevailing interest rates and inflations rate target. Because, in the past and today, banks have abused this power, Bitcoin takes a different approach, by decentralizing the authority of issuing money. Its monetary policy is fixed, publicly known and run by consensus.

Bitcoins money supply is fixed, capped at 21 million bitcoins. There will never be more than this amount in circulation. Regular currency on the other hand, is printed by national at the whims of Central Banks. It is susceptible to interference from the state, political influence and mismanagement.

Virtual currencies are purely digital tokens that represent some value. With this value, they can be exchanged or redeemed for goods, and services. Unlike national currencies that we are accustomed to, such as the Kenya shilling, virtual currencies have no paper form, they exist only in digital form. They are also not issued by national central banks. Corporations, cooperative unions, retail stores, e-commerce giants and online communities have issued most VCs in the recent past.

Bitcoin on the other hand, and cryptocurrencies are a new breed of decentralized digital currencies not issued by a central authority.

Examples of virtual currencies include bonga points, nakumatt points, World of Warcraft coins, Q coins and Bitcoin.
