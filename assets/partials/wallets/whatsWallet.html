Bitcoin wallets are just like traditional leather wallets for holding cash. A bitcoin wallet is where you store your bitcoins. Using a wallet, you can send, receive, and check your balance. Depending on the wallet you choose to use, more features are available such as an address book, transaction history, add a note and more.

There are 3 basic types of wallet

  1. Mobile wallet
  2. Web wallet
  3. Hardware wallet

You need a bitcoin wallet so you can send, store and receive bitcoins from your choice of device. For example a mobile wallet on your phone, a web wallet when you are at your computer or web browser and finally hardware wallets which are the best for storing large amounts of bitcoins for long periods of time

A bitcoin address is similar to an account number, for receiving and sending funds. . Bitcoins are sent from one bitcoin address to another. In every bitcoin wallet, you will find your receiving address. It is a long string of numbers and letters that resembles 3J98t1WpEZ73CNmQviecrnyiWrnqRhWNLy. You share it with your friends, customers, or anyone paying you bitcoin.

If you find Bitcoin addresses to be too long and hard to recall, there is an alternative – QR codes. QR codes are easier way to share, send and receive bitcoins.

Here is an example of QR code

A QR code is an image form that represents your bitcoin addresses. They are scanned to send and receive bitcoins, making them far more easier to use than copy pasting addresses. Like pictures, they can be shared via social media or on your online store or blog.

Scanning a QR code is easy and simple. Depending on the bitcoin wallet you are using, the process may vary. Generally however, it is all the same. Point a camera at a bitcoin QR code and scan to:

  1. Send bitcoins to a receiver or receiving bitcoin address
  2. Enter a bitcoin address in your contact list

Mobile bitcoin wallets will automatically link to your camera from within the app itself. Therefore,

If you want to send bitcoins

  • Launch your mobile wallet
  • Enter password (recommended for security)
  • Go to ‘Send’
  • Go to ‘Scan QR code’

If you want to add a contact to your address book

  • Launch your mobile wallet
  • Enter password (recommended for security)
  • Select ‘Camera’, an option between ‘Send’ and ‘Receive’
  • Scan the QR code to add to contact
  • Label your new contact
  • Save
  • You have now added this bitcoin address into your contact

To send bitcoins using a QR code, ask your intended receiver to show your their bitcoin address QR code format. As the sender, you must have a camera either on your phone, PC laptop or desktop computer.

  • Ask the receiver to show you their QR code
  • Go to ‘Send’ on your wallet
  • Select Scan QR code
  • Scan the QR code of your intended receiver
  • Enter the correct amount to send
  • Confirm the amount to send is correct
  • Press send

*Some web and desktop wallets may not have an option to Scan QR code

To receive bitcoins, share your QR code with your sender. All bitcoin addresses can be represented in QR code format. On most wallets, a QR code version of your bitcoin address will be displayed. The sender then Scans this QR code and sends bitcoins to your wallet.

  1. Go to your mobile wallet
  2. Enter your password (recommended for security)
  3. Go to ‘Receive’
  4. Your bitcoin address and its QR code are displayed
  5. Tap the QR code twice to make it clear
  6. Show your sender your QR code (face to face or via video skype)
  7. Your sender will Scan your QR code and send the amount of bitcoins

  1. Using QR codes saves you the trouble of having to manually enter or copy paste long bitcoin addresses
  2. QR codes can be shared on a blog, profile picture, website, or anywhere online to receive bitcoin payments from anyone
  3. QR codes can be printed out and stuck on physical stores where customers can quickly scan the merchant QR code to pay
  4. You can generate QR codes embedded with a defined amount of bitcoin to be received. For example, a QR code customized to receive 100 Kshs. only. Thus, anyone paying using your QR code only pays an amount defined by you.
  5. Bitcoin QR codes are quick, secure and fun to use when making payments online or payments in person or face to face

Like email and email addresses, bitcoin wallets are made to be easy and simple to use. Some will walk you through the standard way of signing up for online service: ask for a username, set a password and verify via email. Others however, are downloaded and installed on your phone.

To open a bitcoin wallet, the first step is to choose the type of wallet you want.

Fortunately, there a variety type of wallets to pick from.

For mobile wallets

  • Visit google playstore on android
  • Search for one of one of your choice or the recommended wallets
  • Install the wallet on your smartphone device.
  • Launch the wallet application
  • Backup your wallet for security. See how to secure your bitcoins, how to backup your wallet
  • Set a 6 digit PIN password code for your wallet
  • Your wallet is now ready for use

For web wallets

  • Visit a web wallet service like blockchain.info
  • Sign up via email address with username and strong password
  • Verify via email address
  • Login and go to settings
  • Setup 2 Factor Authentication
  • Verify via SMS
  • Your wallet is now ready to use

For Hardware wallets

  • Buy a hardware wallet
  • Follow the simple instructions in your user manual
  • Login to your wallet
  • Confirm that you have a enough balance
  • Go to send option
  • enter the recipient’s address or select from address book
  • Click send
  • Enter your PIN code or password to authorize send
  • A notification will inform you the transaction is complete.
  • Login to your wallet,
  • Go to an option that reads “Give out the bitcoin address below to receive bitcoins”.
  • Copy and share the address generated to receive funds.
  • Alternatively, your wallet can show addresses as as QR codes.
  • Display this image and anyone can scan it to send you bitcoins.
  • A notification will display showing incoming bitcoins in your wallet

The Ethereum blockchain has been around for 11-months, but there are many options for wallets that can hold ether (ETH).

Jaxx

One of the best user friendly options is Jaxx. Jaxx is a multiple asset wallet that can hold both Bitcoin (BTC) and Ethereum (ETH). It is available for download on Android, iOS, accessed via Web or a Chrome-plugin. Jaxx has a great user interface, runs smooth and works across multiple devices. For example you can pair two devices and use your Jaxx wallet across devices from your phone to your PC or your tablet.

Before adding cryptocurrency on your wallet, first back up your wallet and add a PIN.

  1. Go to > Tools > Back Up
  2. Go to > Setting > Set PIN

MyEtherWallet

Another user-centric wallet options is MyEtherWallet This website may be run either from their web browser or locally to create an Ethereum address. On this web page, you can save an encrypted JSON file or write down your private key. Alternatively, you can print out a paper wallet.

We recommend you read up some more on the Help section before you are comfortable.

Other options

For more advanced options go to Ethreum.org to download their Mist client, which will run a full node, or other command-line options for various programming languages like C++, Golang, Rust, and Python.
