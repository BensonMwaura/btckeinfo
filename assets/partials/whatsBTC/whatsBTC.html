Yes! Bitcoins are real. Bitcoin is revolutionary because it is the first true form of a digital commodity. When you hold bitcoin, you actually own them. To help you get a picture, holding bitcoins is like having cash in your pocket, when you hold, no one else can take unless you hand it to them. Bitcoins cannot be taken from you because possession is ownership. In fact, when you own bitcoin, not even the government can take them from you. That is why, it is often referred to as the only censorship resistant asset.

Bitcoin was first released as an open source software by Satoshi Nakamoto in January 2009. This began the Bitcoin network that has massively grown to what we have today.

Satoshi Nakamoto had previously published the Bitcoin white paper ‘Bitcoin a Peer-to-Peer Electronic Cash System’ in October 2008, outlining and describing bitcoin the digital currency.

Satoshi Nakamoto is the name of person or group of people who created Bitcoin, the peer to peer decentralized open network. Satoshi Nakamoto is believed to be a pseudonym for the person or group of people who first published the Bitcoin white paper. There is no clear evidence to confirm whether Satoshi was an individual, man, woman or team.

It is generally accepted that Satoshi Nakamoto was most likely a team of experts who worked together to produce the open source code that is now Bitcoin.

Satoshi’s identity still remains unknown till today. In the early days of Bitcoin, Satoshi was careful to protect their identity and only communicated through encrypted email. Satoshi appeared regularly on and off on forums such as here. Satoshi’s history of communication with the bitcoin community can be found here.

Since 2008, the mysterious identity of the creator of Bitcoin has been a mystery. Several people have claimed to be Satoshi Nakamoto. The media has also attempted to uncover the mystery of Satoshi.

It continues to be a mystery today.

Bitcoin is not anonymous. Bitcoin is pseudonymous.

There is often confusion on whether Bitcoin is anonymous. The truth is that it is not. Every Bitcoin transaction can be viewed publicly on the Bitcoin blockchain. The blockchain has a permanent record of all the transactions that have happened in the entire history of Bitcoin. It is therefore possible, to see all the Bitcoins transactions happening in real time on the public blockchain.

Bitcoin is pseudonymous because, while it is possible to see all the transactions on the Blockchain, the identity of the senders and receivers is not visible. It possible to see bitcoins move from one address to another, but there is no way of telling who is sending or receiving. This is called pseudonymity.
